Old Stars

Michael Jordan

Michael Jordan is the formal shooting guard for the Chicago Bulls. He has been in the NBA for 15 years total. He was elected to the NBA in 1984. He was soon proved on the court, With an average score of 28.2 in his rookie year, he receive the NBA Rookie of The Year award. He received 5 Most Valuable Player Award and helped the team win 6 championships. Although he left basketball for a year to play baseball, he still joined back to the NBA league. When he joined back, he was even stronger than when he left, averaging 30.4 points a game. The following year, Michael Jordan Started all 82 games and set a record of 72 wins in the regular season. Although he is now retired, he was a great legend of the NBA history.

Shaquille O'Neil

Shaquille O’Neal is the formal Center of Boston Celtics(the last team he played for out of 6). He has been in the NBA for about 19 years where he played 4 years in Orlando Magic, 8 years in Los Angeles Lakers, 4 years in Miami Heat, 1 in Phoenix Suns, 1 in Cleveland Cavaliers, and 1 in Boston Celtics. Shaquille became the first player in NBA history to be named “Player of the Week” in his first week in the league. A notoriously bad free- throw shooter, he focused on getting more points on his power slam. He was able to shatter 2 backboards slamming the ball too hardly. He has a total of 4 championships, three in Los Angeles Lakers and one in Miami Heat. Shaquille is currently the commentator of the show Shaqtin a Fool.

Wilt Chamberlain

Wilt Chamberlain if the formal Center of Lakers(the last team he played for out of 3). He has been in the NBA for 13 years where he played 6 years in the Philadelphia Warriors, 3 years in the Philadelphia 76ers, and 4 years in the Los Angeles Lakers. Wilt Chamberlain had great basketball talent. In his first NBA game, he got 43 points. His rookie season he receive the Rookie of the Year as well as getting the MVP award. Wilt Chamberlain was famous for several reasons. The most astonishing one was his 100 point game in his best season. Also during that season, he became the first player to score over 4000 point in the season averaging 50.4 points. During his basketball journey he broke and set 8 NBA records and 3 MVPs. Despite his records, he was only able to get 2 championships for his team. Although Wilt Chamberlain is now passed away, he is someone that is greatly remembered.

Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant is currently the shooting guard of the Los Angeles Lakers. He has played in the NBA for 19 years all in the same team-Los Angeles Lakers. You might be wondering why did I put Kobe in the old star list? This is because Kobe’s aging has made him not as fit then when he first started playing, and that was a decade ago. During Kobe’s second season, he was voted a starter for a all-star game, becoming the youngest all-star of 19 years old in NBA history. He then teamed up with Shaquille O’Neil and won three consecutive champions for the lakers. Kobe has won 5 championships in total. One of Kobe’s most memorable moment is when he scored 81 points in a game, ranking the second highest points in a game. Although Kobe is retiring after the 2015-2016 season, he has left many memorable moments to us.
